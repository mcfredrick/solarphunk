## Phase 1 — Orient & Tag

For each research note, write:
- Primary theme cluster (one of the domains above)
- One tension it raises but doesn't resolve

Then: which theme clusters are over-represented in recent posts? Which are underrepresented?

## Phase 2 — Connect

Identify 3 candidate lateral connections. Each must bridge notes from two DIFFERENT theme clusters. For each candidate:
- Name the two notes
- Describe the bridge
- State the question it opens for the reader

Rank the candidates. The winner is the one most surprising while remaining grounded in real, existing things described in the research notes.

## Phase 3 — Insight

State the single lateral insight for this post in one sentence. It must be:
1. Citable from the research notes above
2. Able to expand what the reader believes is possible
3. Not a repeat of any recent post topic

If it would repeat a recent post, use the second-ranked candidate from Phase 2.

## Phase 4 — Draft

Write the complete post.

Structure:
- Opening (1–2 paragraphs): A concrete, surprising scene or fact grounded in the research. Drop the reader in. No preamble. No "In recent years..."
- Middle (3–5 paragraphs): Develop the lateral insight. Weave in 2–4 research note IDs explicitly. Show the connections; don't just assert them.
- Close (1–2 paragraphs): An open question or image. Send the reader somewhere. Do not summarize. No call to action.
